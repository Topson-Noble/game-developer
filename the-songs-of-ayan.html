The Songs Of Ayan

A narrative-based rhythm game.

Engine: Unreal Engine 5.7.4 Platform: PC (Windows) Role: Solo Developer Focus: Rhythm & Narrative Systems
Download Game View on GitHub

Project Overview

>>> The Songs of Ayan is a short narrative rhythm game where every beat carries meaning and every rhythm challenge has a significance.

>>> The story follows Sayo, a young man of the Ayan lineage, as he undertakes shrine-based rhythm challenges, collects drum artifacts, and seeks the blessings of Ayan Agalu, the Yoruba deity of drumming, in order to complete his initiation into the Ayan drumming guild.

>>> Step into an experience inspired by the Yoruba drumming tradition of southwestern Nigeria, where rhythm is more than sound. It is language, identity, and storytelling. Through a series of rhythm challenges, you will gradually uncover the significance behind each interaction and connect with the spirit of the drum.

>>> The game translates real-life drumming practices into interactive mechanics, embedding cultural meaning directly into gameplay while reinforcing narrative progression.

>>> This project was developed solo and created as part of my master’s thesis in the REPLAY program, and this is the first playable version shared for public playtesting.

>>> The game takes about 10 to 15 minutes to complete, making it a quick but meaningful experience.

Rhythm Gameplay: From Functional to Immersive

The rhythm gameplay began with a simple prototype. While the core mechanics were functional, the presentation felt visually plain and lacked the level of immersion needed to deliver the intended player experience.

In the initial implementation, rhythm notes were generated using Tick events. However, this caused synchronization issues, as the notes would gradually fall out of sync with the background music on different PCs due to varying frame rates. To solve this, I replaced the Tick-based system with Unreal Engine's Quartz Clock system, allowing the notes to be scheduled against the audio timeline instead of the frame rate. This resulted in much more accurate and consistent synchronization across different hardware.

Through several rounds of prototyping and iteration, the rhythm gameplay evolved from a basic functional prototype into a polished experience that better captured the intended feel, both visually and mechanically.

Loop Refinement: From fragmented to cohesive

The early version featured numerous mechanics, including realm travel, platforming, shrine sacrifices, rhythm fragment collection, and drum artifacts. While these systems worked individually, they competed for the player's attention, resulting in a fragmented experience with an unclear gameplay focus.

After several rounds of iteration, the gameplay was streamlined into a more cohesive progression. Environmental exploration naturally leads players to narrative-driven shrines, where rhythm challenges reward new ceremonial drums that unlock the next stage of the journey..

This refinement transformed the game from a collection of disconnected mechanics into a focused gameplay loop where exploration, narrative, and rhythm work together to deliver the intended player experience.

Concept Evolution: From Exploratory to Purpose-Driven

During pre-production, I rapidly prototyped different gameplay concepts, including AI escort-style gameplay, platforming, environmental puzzles, and survival-based rhythm mechanics. Although many of these ideas were promising, they did not fully support the narrative-rhythm experience defined by my thesis.

Rather than forcing every mechanic into a single game, I evaluated each prototype, retained the ideas that best reinforced the core vision, and discarded the rest. This iterative process ultimately shaped the focused gameplay loop found in the final experience.
