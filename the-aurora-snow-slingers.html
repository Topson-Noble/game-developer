Project Overview

The Aurora Snow Slingers is a concise, lighthearted action experience developed for the Aurora Game Jam, part of the REPLAY Game Design Master's Degree Program.

Gameplay Concept

Guided by the theme "Echo of the Polar Night," the project interprets the idea of an echo not as sound alone, but as the reverberation of joyful childhood memories, specifically the bright laughter and spirited energy of kids engaged in a friendly snowball fight beneath the dancing aurora.

The game is designed to evoke a sense of nostalgia, presenting a fictional yet emotionally familiar moment: friends playing in the snow during the long polar night, illuminated by the aurora's movement overhead.

Players enter a circular snow-fight arena populated with snowmen, igloos, and snow walls that provide tactical cover. Within this arena, the game attempts to emulate a simplified form of real-world snowball-fight mechanics. The player can run, collect individual snowballs from the ground, and manually launch them toward an opponent. This design encourages deliberate, skill-based play, requiring each throw to be purposeful rather than relying on rapid, repeated attacks.

Design & Aesthetics

- Visual Style: Vibrant and colorful, with the aurora in the sky to beautify the scenery and also serve as the main source of lighting to the environment.

- Character Design: Character reused for player's character, AI, and background NPCs.

- Audio: A fast-paced action music that drives the energy of the gameplay.

- UI: UI design that clearly communicates to the player.

Development Details

- Engine: Unreal Engine 5.5.4

- Development Focus: Snowball projectiles mechanics; health system; difficulty mode selection tied to AI's behaviour trees logic.

- Role: Responsible for 3d assets creation, game design, and gameplay and UI programming.

- Dev Status: Current prototype is a player-vs-AI mode. Future update to include a player-vs-player mode.

- Platforms: PC (Windows), with potential scalability for web or mobile builds.
