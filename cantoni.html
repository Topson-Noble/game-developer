Project Overview

Cantoni is a lighthearted, casual point-and-click game built around a dynamic leaderboard system. The game was designed as both a fun interactive experience and a personal technical challenge to implement player data persistence and scoring mechanics within a complete gameplay loop.

The title "Cantoni" takes its name from the original soundtrack composed for the game, a lively one-minute track that sets the rhythm and tone for this cheerful, slightly chaotic world where happiness is constantly under attack.

Gameplay Concept

At the start of each session, players enter their name, which the game saves and remembers for later ranking on the leaderboard. Once the game begins, players are introduced to a fictional, upbeat world where three joyful friends dance at the center of the screen, simply trying to stay happy.

Their peace, however, is constantly disrupted by a mischievous bubble-shooting creature that orbits around them, launching bubbles from every direction. The player's sole task is to click on these bubbles before they reach the friends, popping them to protect the group's happiness level.

Each bubble popped earns a score, while missed ones reduce happiness. The challenge continues for the full duration of the Cantoni music track, roughly one minute of fast-paced, rhythmic gameplay. When the music ends, the game stops spawning bubbles and transitions to a results screen showing the player's final score and ranking.

Leaderboard & Progression

One of the game's key features is its persistent leaderboard system.

After every session:

- The player's name and score are automatically saved.

- Scores are ranked in real-time, showing only the top 10 players, the "Top Poppers".

- Returning players can see their new scores compared with previous ones, encouraging replayability and friendly competition.

The leaderboard implementation was the project's primary learning goal, showcasing data saving, player tracking, and score sorting logic within a self-contained Unreal Engine project.

Design & Aesthetics

- Visual Style: Simple and minimal color, focusing on clarity of motion for quick reaction gameplay.

- Character Design: Three expressive, looping characters represent joy and friendship at the game's heart.

- Antagonist: A spiky creature that moves cyclically along the screen's edges, dynamically spawning bubbles toward the center.

- Audio: Original background track "Cantoni" composed to synchronize with gameplay pacing, giving the experience rhythm and personality.

- UI: Clean interface for player input, score tracking, and leaderboard display.

Development Details

- Engine: Unreal Engine 5.5.4

- Development Focus: Gameplay systems, UI/UX, and persistent leaderboard integration.

- Role: Solo developer, responsible for game design, programming, sound composition, and visual direction.

- Platforms: PC (Windows), with potential scalability for web or mobile builds.

Technical & Creative Highlights

- Implemented a custom leaderboard system with persistent score saving and real-time ranking.

- Created a data-driven player naming and scoring system with memory retention between sessions.

- Designed cyclic enemy AI that orbits the scene and spawns projectiles dynamically.

- Built timed gameplay flow synchronized with the original Cantoni soundtrack.

- Composed and produced the game's theme music, integrating it directly into gameplay rhythm and feedback.

- Focused on polished UX, ensuring the leaderboard, animations, and end-state transitions feel smooth and responsive.

Reflection

Cantoni represents a balance of creative design and technical problem-solving, combining playful, accessible gameplay with system-level functionality like data persistence and score management.

This project allowed me to explore player engagement through competition, while refining skills in UI scripting, event logic, and timing-based gameplay, all within a tight, self-contained development cycle.
